# HR Analytics Dashboard: Attrition & Workforce Overview

This dashboard analyzes **employee attrition and workforce demographics** to help HR teams identify turnover patterns and potential retention risks.

The visualizations highlight trends across departments, demographics, and job satisfaction levels to support data-driven workforce decisions.

---

## Key Performance Indicators

| Metric | Value |
|------|------|
| Employee Count | 1,470 |
| Attrition Count | 237 |
| Attrition Rate | 16.12% |
| Active Employees | 1,233 |
| Average Age | 37 |

---

## Core Visualizations

### Attrition Breakdown

- **Department-wise Attrition:**  
  The **R&D department** has the highest attrition (133 employees, 56%), followed by **Sales** (92 employees).

- **Education Field Attrition:**  
  Employees with **Life Sciences** and **Medical** backgrounds show the highest departure counts.

- **Attrition by Gender:**  
  Male attrition (150) is higher than female attrition (87).

---

### Workforce Demographics

- **Employee Age Distribution:**  
  Most employees fall within the **30–36 age range**.

- **Attrition by Gender & Age Group:**  
  The **25–34 age group** shows the highest turnover rates for both genders.

---

### Job Satisfaction Analysis

A matrix visualization highlights job satisfaction scores (1–4) across roles.

| Job Role | High Satisfaction (4) | Total Employees |
|--------|--------|--------|
| Sales Executive | 112 | 326 |
| Research Scientist | 95 | 292 |
| Laboratory Technician | 80 | 259 |
| Healthcare Representative | 43 | 131 |

---

## Key Insights

- **High-Risk Department:** R&D shows the highest employee turnover.
- **Critical Age Group:** Employees aged **25–34** demonstrate the highest attrition rates.
- **Role Variability:** Job satisfaction varies significantly across roles, suggesting potential improvement areas in workplace engagement.
